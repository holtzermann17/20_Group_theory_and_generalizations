subgroups of finite cyclic group


Let n be the order of a finite cyclic group G.  For every positive divisor (http://planetmath.org/Divisibility) m of n, there exists one and only one subgroup of order m of G.  The group G has no other subgroups.

Proof.  If g is a generator of G and  n=m⁢k,  then gk generates the subgroup ⟨gk⟩, the order of which is equal to the order of gk, i.e. equal to m.  Any subgroup H of G is cyclic (see http://planetmath.org/node/4097this entry).  If  |H|=m,  then H must have a generator of order m; thus apparently  H=⟨g±k⟩=⟨gk⟩.
